### **Part 1: Lecture Notes (Beyond Enterprise Architecture)**

The role of an Enterprise Architect is constantly evolving. To stay relevant, you must integrate these future trends into your architectural planning.

#### **► Future Trends driving EA**
1.  **Cloud Computing & SaaS:** Moving from on-premise data centers to flexible, scalable cloud solutions (AWS, Azure).
2.  **Big Data & AI:**
    *   **Data Science/Analytics:** Understanding the past.
    *   **AI/Machine Learning:** Predicting the future and automating decisions.
3.  **Customer Experience (CX):** Shifting focus from "System efficiency" to "Customer satisfaction" across all industries (Retail, Banking, etc.).
4.  **Digital Transformation:** Fundamentally changing how business operates (not just digitizing paper, but changing the business model).
5.  **IoT & Blockchain:** Automation and secure, decentralized ledgers.

#### **► Future-Proofing your Architecture**
How to ensure your architecture doesn't become obsolete?
*   **Feedback Loops:** Actively seek feedback from employees and customers.
*   **Training:** Invest in continuous learning for IT staff.
*   **Scan the Horizon:** Implement mechanisms to identify external trends (like AI shifts) before they disrupt you.
*   **Agility:** Architect systems to be modular so they can adopt new technologies quickly.

### **Part 2: Course Summary (The "Cheat Sheet" View)**

If you need to visualize the whole course in your head for the exam, here is the structure:

*   **Module I: The Basics**
    *   **What is EA?** Aligning IT with Business Strategy.
    *   **Frameworks:** TOGAF is the standard.
*   **Module II: The ADM Core (Designing)**
    *   **Phase A (Vision):** The "Why" and "Where".
    *   **Phase B (Business):** The "Who" and "What" (Processes, Org structure). *Most important phase.*
    *   **Phase C (Info Systems):** The "How" (Data & Applications).
    *   **Phase D (Technology):** The "With What" (Infrastructure, Hardware).
*   **Module III: The ADM Realisation (Planning & Building)**
    *   **Phase E (Opportunities):** The Roadmap.
    *   **Phase F (Migration):** The Project Plan (Time, Cost, Risk).
    *   **Phase G (Governance):** Ensuring compliance during coding/building.
    *   **Phase H (Change):** Managing updates after go-live.
    *   **Requirements Mgmt:** The center circle driving everything.
*   **Module IV: The Practice (People)**
    *   **Capability:** Setting up the EA team.
    *   **Maturity:** Moving from "Ad-hoc" to "Optimized".
    *   **Skills:** Soft skills (selling ideas) are as important as hard skills.
