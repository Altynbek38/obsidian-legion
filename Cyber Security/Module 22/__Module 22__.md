### **Module 22: Governance, Risk, & Compliance - Exam Notes**

#### **1. Security Governance vs. Management**

*   **Governance:** The strategic oversight. Determines **who** is authorized to make decisions and ensures security strategies align with business goals and compliance.
*   **Management:** The tactical implementation. Defines and applies the **controls** to mitigate risks.

**Key Data Roles:**

| Role | Responsibility |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Data Owner** | **Accountable.** Classifies data and determines access criteria. |
| **Data Custodian** | **Technical.** Implements the security controls defined by the owner (e.g., backups, patching). |
| **Data Controller** | Determines the **purpose** and means of processing data. |
| **Data Processor** | Processes data on behalf of the controller. |

---

#### **2. Cybersecurity Policies**

*   **Policy Structure:**
    1.  **Master Policy:** The strategic blueprint.
    2.  **System-Specific:** Standards for hardware/software (hardening).
    3.  **Issue-Specific:** Rules for specific situations (e.g., Cloud usage).
*   **Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):** Critical document defining the rules for using network resources and the consequences of violations.
*   **HR Principles:** Background checks, "Need to know" access, Separation of duties, and Job rotation (to prevent fraud).

---

#### **3. Ethics & Cybercrime**

**Ethical Approaches:**
*   **Utilitarian:** Focuses on **consequences**. The choice that provides the greatest good for the greatest number.
*   **Rights:** Focuses on individual **choices** and fundamental rights (Privacy, Truth).
*   **Common Good:** Focuses on the benefit to the **community**.

**Cybercrime Categories:**
*   **Computer-Targeted:** The computer is the victim (e.g., DDoD, Malware).
*   **Computer-Assisted:** The computer is a tool (e.g., Fraud, Theft).
*   **Computer-Incidental:** The computer holds evidence (e.g., Contraband storage).

#### **5. ISO 27000 Framework**

*   **ISO 27000 Series:** A universal standard for **Information Security Management Systems (ISMS)**. It uses a "Peer Model" (12 interconnected domains), not layers like OSI.
*   **Control Objectives (ISO 27001):** The checklist of **"What"** needs to be achieved (High-level requirements).
*   **Controls (ISO 27002):** The guidelines on **"How"** to implement them.
*   **Statement of Applicability (SOA):** Used to tailor controls based on an organization's priorities regarding the **CIA Triad**:
    *   *Example:* **Amazon** prioritizes **Availability** (Sales). **Google** prioritizes **Confidentiality** (User trust).

---

#### **6. Audits & Other Frameworks**

*   **SSAE 18 / SOC 2 Audit:** An independent audit for service providers.
    *   **Type I:** Snapshot at a specific point in time.
    *   **Type II:** Managed over a period (min. 6 months).
*   **CIS Controls:** Prioritized actions for cyber defense (Basic, Foundational, Organizational).
*   **CSA CCM (Cloud Controls Matrix):** The standard framework for cloud security assurance.
*   **CMMC:** Certification levels required for **DoD (Department of Defense)** contractors.
