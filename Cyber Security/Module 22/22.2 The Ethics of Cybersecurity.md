
# **The Ethics of Cybersecurity**

**Ethics** acts as an internal guide for right and wrong. A cybersecurity specialist must balance the law, the organization's interests, and ethical responsibility.

### **Ethical Perspectives**

| Approach | Principle |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Utilitarian** | Focuses on consequences. The ethical choice provides the **greatest good for the greatest number** of people. |
| **Rights** | Focuses on individual rights. Decisions must respect rights to privacy, truth, and safety. |
| **Common Good** | Focuses on the community. Actions should benefit the community as a whole and shared values. |

### **The Ten Commandments of Computer Ethics**
*Created by the Computer Ethics Institute.*

1.  Do not use a computer to **harm** other people.
2.  Do not **interfere** with other people's computer work.
3.  Do not **snoop** around in other people's computer files.
4.  Do not use a computer to **steal**.
5.  Do not use a computer to **bear false witness**.
6.  Do not use proprietary software for which you have **not paid** (piracy).
7.  Do not use other people's resources without **authorization**.
8.  Do not **appropriate** other people's intellectual output.
9.  Think about the **social consequences** of the program you are writing.
10. Use a computer in ways that ensure **consideration and respect** for others.

# **Cybercrime Categories**

| Category | Definition | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Computer-Targeted** | The computer is the victim/target of the attack. | Malware, DoS, Hacking. |
| **Computer-Assisted** | The computer is a tool used to commit a crime. | Theft, Fraud. |
| **Computer-Incidental** | The computer is not the tool or target, but holds evidence. | Storing illegally downloaded videos/contraband. |

---

# **Cyber Law Classifications (USA)**

1.  **Statutory Law:** Laws passed by Congress (Legislative). Includes civil and criminal penalties (e.g., Computer Fraud and Abuse Act).
2.  **Administrative Law:** Regulations enforced by government agencies (e.g., FCC, FTC) to ensure compliance.
3.  **Common Law:** Based on judicial precedent and previous court rulings.

# **Key Cybersecurity Laws & Acts**

### **Federal & Industry Standards**

| Law / Act | Sector | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **FISMA** | **Federal Agencies** | Requires federal agencies to implement an info security program (risk assessment, annual inventory, training). |
| **GLBA** | **Finance** | (Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act) Protects private data in financial institutions; restricts sharing with 3rd parties. |
| **SOX** | **Accounting** | (Sarbanes-Oxley Act) Overhauled corporate accounting standards for public firms to prevent fraud. |
| **PCI DSS** | **Credit Cards** | (Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard) Contractual rules for protecting cardholder data. Violations result in fines or loss of processing ability. |

### **Privacy & Data Protection**

| Law / Act | Focus | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **CFAA** | **Hacking** | (Computer Fraud and Abuse Act) Prohibits unauthorized access to computers (hacking/trespassing). |
| **ECPA** | **Communication** | (Electronic Communications Privacy Act) Protects against unauthorized interception of email and telephone conversations. |
| **Privacy Act (1974)**| **Gov Records** | Governs how federal agencies handle PII (Personally Identifiable Information). |
| **FOIA** | **Public Access** | (Freedom of Information Act) Allows public access to government records, unless exempted (e.g., National Security). |
| **FERPA** | **Education** | Protects student education records. Parents must opt-in for disclosure until the student is 18. |
| **COPPA** | **Minors (<13)** | Requires parental consent before collecting info from children under 13 online. |
| **CIPA** | **Minors (<17)** | Requires libraries/schools to filter offensive internet content. |
| **VPPA** | **Video History** | Prevents disclosure of video rental/viewing history without consent (e.g., Netflix). |
| **HIPAA** | **Healthcare** | National standards for protecting electronic health information (security & privacy). |
| **SB 1386** | **Breach Notification** | (California Law) The first state law requiring organizations to notify individuals if their personal data is breached. |

### **International Law**

*   **Convention on Cybercrime:** The first international treaty seeking to address internet crimes (copyright, fraud, child pornography, network security violations). Ratified by 65 states.

# **Compliance Tools**

*   **Privacy Policy:** Ensures organizational compliance by stating how data is collected and used.
*   **Privacy Impact Assessment (PIA):** A process to identify how PII is handled, review legal requirements, and identify risks.
