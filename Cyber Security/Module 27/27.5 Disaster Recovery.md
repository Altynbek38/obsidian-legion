## **2. Disaster Recovery Plan (DRP)**
Activities to assess, salvage, repair, and restore damaged facilities/assets **during** a disaster.

*   **Key Question:** What are the most **Critical Systems**? (Prioritize restoring these first).
*   **DRP Controls:**
    *   **ğŸ›‘ Preventive:** Identify and mitigate risks to prevent disaster.
    *   **ğŸ” Detective:** Uncover new threats/unwanted events (e.g., Alarms).
    *   **ğŸ› ï¸ Corrective:** Restore systems *after* the event.

## **3. Business Continuity Planning (BCP)**
A broader plan than DRP. Focuses on **keeping the business running**, potentially at an alternate location, until normal operations resume.

### **Business Impact Analysis (BIA)**
The first step of BCP. Identifies critical processes and the consequences of interruption.

**Key Metrics:**
*   **RTO (Recovery Time Objective):** Maximum tolerable downtime.
*   **RPO (Recovery Point Objective):** Maximum acceptable data loss (measured in time, e.g., "loss of 1 hour of data").
*   **MTTR (Mean Time To Repair):** Average time to fix a failed component.
*   **MTBF (Mean Time Between Failures):** Average time between one failure and the next.

> [!important] **NIST Best Practices for BCP:**
> 1.  Develop Policy.
> 2.  Conduct BIA.
> 3.  Calculate Risk.
> 4.  Identify Preventive Controls.
> 5.  Develop Recovery Strategies.
> 6.  Develop Contingency Plan.
> 7.  **Test the Plan.**
> 8.  Maintain/Update the Plan.
